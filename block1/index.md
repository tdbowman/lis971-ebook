# Block 1: Research Design + Sampling 🟢

**March 16 – March 29 | 15% of course grade**

> *What is your research approach, and who are your participants?*

## Block Overview

Block 1 is where you lay the foundation for your entire methodology. The decisions you make here — your philosophical stance, your choice of research design, and your sampling strategy — will determine the logic of everything that follows. Every subsequent choice about data collection, analysis, and quality criteria must be *coherent* with the decisions you make in this block.

## What You'll Learn

By the end of Block 1, you should be able to:

- Articulate your philosophical worldview and its implications for methodology
- Choose and defend a research design (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed methods)
- Specify a rigorous sampling strategy appropriate to your design and questions
- Identify and address anticipated challenges in your research plan

## Block 1 Deliverable

**Research Design Overview + Sampling Strategy** (4–6 pages)

Your deliverable must include:

1. Research questions
2. Methodological approach and justification
3. Population description
4. Sampling strategy with specific procedures
5. Anticipated challenges
