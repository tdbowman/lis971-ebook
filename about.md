# About This Book

This interactive eBook was created for **LIS 971: Research Methods for Dissertation Work** at Dominican University's School of Information Studies.

## Course Context

LIS 971 is the second of two doctoral-level research methods courses in the PhD in Library and Information Science program. Building on LIS 969 (Doctoral Research Methods & Design I, taught by Prof. Malenfant), this course shifts from *understanding and appraising* research to *designing and defending* your own original methodology.

The course addresses qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods approaches to rigorous investigation and problem resolution, with the practical goal of producing a dissertation-quality methodology chapter.

## How This eBook Is Organized

The eBook follows the course's **three-block structure**, with each block building a component of your final methodology chapter:

```{list-table} Block Structure
:header-rows: 1
:widths: 10 25 25 15

* - Block
  - Focus
  - You'll Produce
  - Weight
* - 1
  - Research Design + Sampling
  - Research approach & sampling plan (4–6 pp.)
  - 15%
* - 2
  - Data Collection Protocols
  - Complete, ready-to-implement protocols
  - 20%
* - 3
  - Analysis Plan
  - Detailed analysis procedures (5–7 pp.)
  - 20%
* - Final
  - Methodology Chapter
  - Integrated chapter (12–18 pp.)
  - 20%
```

Each block section contains:
- **Conceptual chapters** synthesizing assigned readings
- **Interactive notebooks** with computational tools you can adapt
- **Exercises** tied directly to your block deliverables

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course and eBook, you will be able to:

- **Design** a comprehensive, defensible research methodology appropriate for your dissertation topic
- **Evaluate** methodological choices across quantitative, qualitative, and mixed-methods approaches
- **Develop** detailed research instruments, protocols, and analysis plans
- **Critique** research designs across diverse methodological traditions
- **Integrate** multiple data sources or methods when appropriate
- **Articulate** and defend methodological decisions using scholarly literature
- **Provide** substantive peer feedback on research designs outside your primary approach

## PhD Student Learning Outcomes

This course maps primarily to PhD SLOs #1, #2, and #5, with additional connections to #3 and #4.
